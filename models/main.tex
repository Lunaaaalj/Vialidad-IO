\section{Problem Description}

We model a four-way traffic intersection as a
\textbf{Constraint Satisfaction Problem} (CSP).
The goal is to find all valid signal configurations that permit
vehicle flow without conflicts.

\section{CSP Formulation}

A CSP is defined as a triple $\langle \mathcal{X},\, \mathcal{D},\, \mathcal{C} \rangle$ where:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\mathcal{X}$ is a finite set of variables,
  \item $\mathcal{D}$ is a function assigning a domain to each variable,
  \item $\mathcal{C}$ is a set of constraints over the variables.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Variables}

Let $\mathcal{X} = \{L_N,\; L_S,\; L_E,\; L_W\}$, where each variable
$L_i$ represents the signal state of direction $i \in \{N, S, E, W\}$.

\subsection{Domains}

Each variable takes a binary value:
\[
  \mathcal{D}(L_i) = \{0,\; 1\} \quad \forall\, i \in \{N, S, E, W\}
\]
where $0 \equiv \text{Red}$ and $1 \equiv \text{Green}$.

\subsection{Constraints}

\begin{constraint}[No crossing conflict: N--E]
North and East cannot both be green simultaneously, since their flows
cross perpendicularly:
\[
  L_N + L_E \leq 1
\]
\end{constraint}

\begin{constraint}[No crossing conflict: N--W]
\[
  L_N + L_W \leq 1
\]
\end{constraint}

\begin{constraint}[No crossing conflict: S--E]
\[
  L_S + L_E \leq 1
\]
\end{constraint}

\begin{constraint}[No crossing conflict: S--W]
\[
  L_S + L_W \leq 1
\]
\end{constraint}

\begin{constraint}[Compatibility: N--S]
North and South flow in opposite directions along the same axis and
are compatible:
\[
  L_N + L_S \leq 2 \quad (\text{no restriction})
\]
\end{constraint}

\begin{constraint}[Compatibility: E--W]
East and West are similarly compatible:
\[
  L_E + L_W \leq 2 \quad (\text{no restriction})
\]
\end{constraint}

\begin{constraint}[Liveness]
At least one direction must be green at all times to ensure the
intersection is not deadlocked:
\[
  L_N + L_S + L_E + L_W \geq 1
\]
\end{constraint}

\section{Feasibility vs.\ Optimization}

The CSP seeks \emph{feasibility}: any assignment
$\mathbf{x} = (L_N, L_S, L_E, L_W) \in \{0,1\}^4$
satisfying $\mathcal{C}$ is a valid solution.
There is \textbf{no objective function}.

If one wished to extend this to an \textbf{Integer Program} (IP)
maximizing throughput, the objective would be:
\[
  \max \quad L_N + L_S + L_E + L_W
\]
subject to Constraints 1--7.
The IP optimal is $z^* = 2$, achieved by exactly two compatible
phases: $\{L_N, L_S\}$ or $\{L_E, L_W\}$.

\section{Solution Space}

The search space has $|\mathcal{D}|^{|\mathcal{X}|} = 2^4 = 16$ candidate
assignments. After enforcing $\mathcal{C}$, the valid configurations are:

\[
\begin{array}{cccc|l}
L_N & L_S & L_E & L_W & \text{Valid?} \\
\hline
0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & \checkmark \\
0 & 0 & 1 & 0 & \checkmark \\
0 & 0 & 1 & 1 & \checkmark \\
0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & \checkmark \\
0 & 1 & 0 & 1 & \checkmark \\
0 & 1 & 1 & 0 & \checkmark \\
1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \checkmark \\
1 & 0 & 0 & 1 & \checkmark \\
1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & \checkmark \\
1 & 1 & 0 & 0 & \checkmark \\
\end{array}
\]

There are \textbf{10 valid configurations} out of 16 candidates.
